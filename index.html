profile

GJ Hilton is, on several levels, one of the country's most unconventional choreographers.

GJH cites his formative influences as George Balanchine, Ian Curtis and John McCarthy†, and the work triangulates these, with an emphasis on rigour of formal conception and execution, a bleak theatricality, but finally, a faith in beauty.
(† as well as Jim Henson, but let's be realistic, nobody's in his league.)

Working increasingly within the vocabulary of classical ballet, the company's work nevertheless incorporates robotics, site-specific staging, biofeedback and custom real-time computer systems. GJH also collaborates extensively as a director and scenographer, as well as a (five times BIMA award-winning) commercial interactive art director.

history

In 1981, GJH built the robot to the left. It was made of cardboard and powered by make believe.

In 1997, GJH accidentally started a dance company. He's been trying to figure it out ever since.

The company's work has been seen internationally, helped define the field of digital performance, and was nominated for a Paul Hamlyn Award for visual art.

Collaborations with Igloo, Sue MacLennan, Darkin Ensemble and others have toured nationally and internationally to four continents, and have been performed at the Royal Opera House, Place Prize, and Monaco Palais des Festivals.
